PBR Study

Art Material Octane PBR

Project Description

To delve deeper into and get more used to rendering in 3D, I decided to look into physically based rendering and conduct a study on this topic. All these different materials were built and rendered using Octane. Post-Production and final adjustments were done in Photoshop. The materials of choice are porcelain, brass and wood for the diffuse materials. The translucent materials consist of crystal, pink / turquoise translucent and a colored jade material.

Models for the materials were experimented with for optimal looks and chosen after some iterations. Different textures were combined via blending to add more details to the material. This study was done under the guidance of Ahmed Joldic and the models were sourced from Sketchfab / Three D Scans.

Key Features

  • Physically Based Rendering

    Analyzing real-life materials and building them from scratch to understand how they came to be, proves not only to be quite interesting, but also fun

  • Material Variety

    Different materials fit some models better than others - trying out several combinations of lighting, material and model is the key to finding the best looking set

  • Mix & Match

    Blending textures often yields unexpected results in terms of overall looks and also opens up the possibility of innovation
