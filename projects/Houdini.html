Houdini Projects

Art Houdini Mantra Simulation

Project Description

As a graduate game development and programming student, Houdini as a software felt like a perfect fit for my wide array of tools for creating digital art. Its coding, data driven and procedural approach to content creation helps tremendeously in this modern age and is a good starting point for any programmer looking to learn more from the art side of things. The topic that interested me the most in all of Houdini are simulations - as an exercise I started an R&D project to create a logo reveal animation using the different available DOPs in Houdini to figure out which look fits me as a tech artist the best.

Furthermore I worked on a fruit clash animation and combined it with a Vellum simulation to create a fun little "satisfying" project and also play around with a new field of simulation. All these projects were created from scratch in Houdini, use Mantra for rendering and were post-produced in After Effects. Fruit models are taken from Fab, but they were adjusted during the project.

Key Features

  • Data-driven Creation

    With millions of points being manipulated and considered for calculations on a frame by frame basis, having complete control over what happens to every single entity also gives you control over every single detail in the simulation

  • Procedural Workflow

    Being able to go back and for example adjust the model of a simulation, just for it to be automatically adjusted to all steps further down the nodes, helps with project iterations and creating templates for specific visual effects

  • High Quality Renders

    Houdini provides support for several different render engines so the final output is fully customizable to ones personal preferences
